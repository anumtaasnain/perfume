Perfume is a fragrant liquid typically composed of a mixture of essential oils, aroma compounds, solvents, and fixatives that are used to give the human body, objects, or living spaces a pleasant and lasting scent. The word "perfume" comes from the Latin word "per fumum," which means "through smoke," reflecting the ancient practice of burning aromatic substances for their fragrance.
